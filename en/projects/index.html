In Bizjapan, members engage with their interests in the form of “projects.”

“Projects” are done in various ways. Sometimes it can be hosting a workshop aimed towards people outside the community or an event co-hosted with companies. On the other hand it can be a study tour involving many associations or providing workshops exclusively for our members. The driving force of initiating projects is not a simple “what to do” but rather an intensive process of thinking “what kind of society is ideal for us to live in”. Each project focuses on creating and carrying out their plans to realize it.

In Bizjapan, every member can initiate a project. They start a project to channel their interests and awareness towards social issues. Bizjapan, full of drilling insights and diversity, has always been holding unique projects covering various disciplines. Many of which are investigating space medicine, providing job hunting support for international students, fostering global interaction through Sado, conducting R&D of pizza ovens and in data science, providing internships for those with low educational attainment, increasing awareness towards Chinese companies, discovering a new self-sufficient Africa, and many more.

ART JUNCTION

~ Destroying the preconception about art ~

Project Leader: Yuka Uemura

Vision

We are convinced that by experiencing art by ourselves, our daily life will become more colorful. Also we believe that anyone can get involved with art by means of of appreciating or expressing it. Therefore, we want to create an environment where one could find an optimum way to get involved with art.

What we did

Under the motivation that more people should be given a chance to think about the link between art and oneself, we convey high school and university students information of young artists from various fields. This is done through conducting interviews and sharing about them on articles and SNS.

Chadō ~The Way of Life~

~ Make Chadō close and approachable ~

Project Leader: Rei Fujishiro

Vision

We want to get rid of Sado’s conventional image of being “hard to join” and “prudish,” and instead share the multi-faceted appeals of Sado. We will spread Sado as a catalyst for spending a happy life, and by doing so, we aim to boost the number of Sado fans in the world.

What we did

Members gather regularly and share knowledge about Chadō and together think what we can do with it. Since Chadō has many different aspects within itself, we plan and hold events and other activities focusing on each one of those aspects (e.g. Chadō × mountaineering, online lectures, partnership with universities abroad, and bringing Chadō in to workplaces).

Japan Trip

~ Transmitting familiar but interesting facts about Japan ~

Project Leader: Akari Doi

Vision

Boosting the number of people interested in Japan as well as Japan fans through uploading videos about Japanese words and interesting aspects of Japan to SNS.

What we did

We take videos where members explain conversational phrases in Japanese that cannot be learnt from textbooks. Jump Japan

~ Providing a choice called life in Japan for every person in the world ~

Project Leader: Aya Shimamura

Vision

Despite the fact that in other countries, there are many students studying Japanese or interested in Japan, not so many international students actually go study in Japan. One cause for this is the lack of information. In order to solve this problem, we provide them with first-hand information on studying in Japan, and by doing so, we wish to increase the number of international students in Japanese universities.

What we did

To non-Japanese students who are considering studying abroad or working in Japan, we provide first-hand information from seniors who have already flown to Japan. Making use of the spring and summer vacation, members fly abroad and collaborate with local student organizations and universities to hold events. In the near future, we are planning to conduct online interview and essay correction after each event.

M x Space

~ Popularizing space medicine ~

Project Leader: Takuma Ishibashi

Vision

We aim for a world where human beings can enjoy high QOSL (Quality Of Space Life). Despite the fact that space medicine is absolutely necessary for the coming age of space traveling, this field is still little-known. We would like to tackle this problem by fostering the development of future human resources in space medicine and interaction between aspiring students.

What we did

The main program of M × Space is a “Space Medicine Study Tour” that is held during spring and summer vacation. During the tour, approximately 5~10 medical students will visit doctors who are active in the field of space medicine, and then listen to their lectures and discuss their accomplishments as well as career path. The professionals we will meet include astronauts, basic researchers, and flight surgeons. Not only that, we will visit special facilities and undergo exercises provided exclusively for this program. By doing so, medical students will get hands-on experience about space medicine and open up their eyes towards a whole new world. The 1st study tour was held on Spring 2018 and enjoyed favorable reputation. The 2nd tour will be held on Summer 2018. This program will be held regularly.

Bizjapan Summit

Activity Year: 2012 - 2015

Vision

Letting international students know about Japan's entrepreneurship as its potential and at the same time providing an opportuniry for Japanese students to interact with top-notch students from abroad.

What we did

The 7-day program was held every year during the 2012-2015 period, and over 120 international students had paticipated. The theme have changed every year. In 2012 it was "Creating a New Business in Tokyo;" in 2013, "Creating a New Business with 'Pop Culture' as a Key Concept;" in 2014, "Formulating a New Enterprise that leads to Tōhoku region's restoration;" in 2015, "A New Social Business Model." In order to prepare themselves for the contest held in the course of the program, participants did various things. They visited Japanese startups such as Bizreach, Terra Motors, AgIC (current Elephantech), pixiv, Euglena; they also experienced Japanese culture, such as sado and judo; they sometimes went to Tohoku region and conducted a fieldwork. All these contributed to an increased understanding towards Japan's business and culture.

Silicon Valley Summer Entrepreneur Camp (SV-SEC)

Activity Year: 2012

Vision

Making Japanese student entrepreneurs get first-hand experience at Silicon Valley, and provide them the experience and opportunity that helps them realize their business plans.

What we did

This program received the sponsorship of Cyber Agent, Inc. After a business contest held in Japan, 7 people from 3 groups were selected out of 150 people from 60 groups to be sent to Silicon Valley from August 4th to 21st in 2012. They visited offices of Facebook, Apple, Twitter, and IDEO; received lectures by Garry Tan, a Y Combinator partner; and participated in hackathons together with local engineers. Participants include mana.bo (current Sundai group) and Azcrap (current Azit).

TriHack Hackathon

Activity Year: 2012

Vision

This program was a competition for qualifying students who will participate as delegates from Japan in a Silicon Valley tour in March 2013 held by Stanford University’s student society ASES (Asia-Pacific Student Entrepreneurship Society).

What we did

This program was held simultaneously in Japan, China, and Korea via internet under the theme, “developing web services or apps that will used widely in Asia,” on September 8 and 9 in 2012. Bizjapan was in charge of management in Japan. The competition was highly competitive, and participants from Japan included entrepreneurs and a “super creator” qualified by Information-technology Promotion Agency.

We can blow our mind away! in Takayama

Activity Year: 2013 - 2015

Vision

Allowing local students in a rural area (Takayama, Gifu Prefecture) to find seniors who can become their role model and to find the potential within themselves which otherwise cannot be found due to limited access to high education and other opportunities.

What we did

The event was held annually from March 2014- March 2016 at Takayama, Gifu Prefecture with local students and their guardians as the main target. There have been approximately 500 participants in total. Each round was composed with a presentation by Bizjapan members about their own experiences, talk session between university students and local students, and individual consultation. According to the post-event questionnaire, the percentage of participants who answered "very satisfied" by the program was more than 98%, which shows how significant this program was. We believe this project helped reduce the information divide between urban and rural areas at least partially.

Oculus-Project (SV-SEC 2015)

Activity Year: 2014

Vision

Increasing the number of developers in the field of Head-Mounted Display (HMD) such as OculusDK2, which its market is estimated to grow very large, and allowing students to create interesting virtual reality contents with their flexible imagination.

What we did

This project consists of two sections. One was the HMD software development lessons and contest held from Late 2014 to April 2015. The other was a one-week Silicon Valley tour in August 2015 for outstanding achievers during the contest. During the HMD software development lessons, we actually developed a software using Unity under the technological support of Dverse.inc and Asukalabo.inc. After we finished with all the lessons, we held a software development contest. The five most prestigious high school and university student engineers were sent to Silicon Valley for a week, and visited local startups and VCs, participated in a meetup with top engineers and local entrepreneurs, and also observed pitch contests.

Bizjapan Change Makers Program

Activity Year: 2016

Vision

Taking the first step to be a change maker by learning steps of entrepreneurship which Bizjapan defines as "Find problems; Plan solusions; then Carry out it". This is done through a 7 days program with its "technolocal" themes.

What we did

The 7-day program was held in Tokyo and Sakura city in Tochigi prefecture in 2016, in which 13 international students participated. During the initial 3 days in Tokyo, participants received a lecture on Japanese entrepreneurship after World War II by Seiichiro Yonekura, an honorary professor of Hitotsubashi University, and listened to a panel discussion on regional revitalization by NPOs and companies involved. They also deepened their understanding on Japanese technology through a visit to the National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology. On day 4 and 5, participants visited Sakura City in Tochigi prefecture, and collaborated with local organizations in fieldwork on the current situation in rural areas. On day 6 and 7, they made group presentations on service and product solutions they had come up with to tackle problems in rural areas of Japan. Through the 7-day program, participants learned the first two steps of being a Change Maker, which is 1. Identifying the issue, and 2. Creating a solution (with the last step being implementation of the solution)

Younger's Bridge(Taiwan, Philippine)

Activity Year: 2016

Vision

New Bizjapan members visited partner organisation abroad to build up good relationship and provided opportunity for new members to broaden their horizons by interacting with intenational students and visiting local companies.

What we did

In the summer of 2017, Bizjapan members visited Taiwan and the Philippines. In Taiwan, members deepened their friendships with members of BIZTaiwan, which is a partner of Bizjapan. Also. we learned about startup cultures of Taiwan by visiting 2 IT startups, VMFive and FlyingV. In the Philippines, we interacted with members of ASES Manila, which is also a partner of Bizjapan, and learned more about the society of the Philippines by visiting places such as historical districts and slums. Also, we visited the Manila office of RareJob Philippines,Inc, which runs an online English learning service, and a studio of CNN Philippines. At the end of this program, we visited Phnom, an area struck by a typhoon in 2013, and exchanged ideas with a member of Gaward Kalinga regarding the sustainable development of communities. This discussion provided us with insights for creating a more “Ideal society”, which cannot be realized solely via economic growth.

UK Japan Student Conference

Activity Year: 2016

Vision

To build a hub for liberal academic discussion amongst youth in the UK and Japan where they can exchange ideas, broaden their vision, and establish long-lasting friendships.

What we did

The first UK-Japan Student Conference was held in London from 10th to 16th of September 2016, under the support of Bizjapan. Focusing on the poverty problems in Japan and the UK, participants analyzed the current situation and causes of these problems and discussed what kind of anti-poverty policies and efforts may be effective. While Japan and the UK are both developed countries and welfare states, their cultures, religions and the views they have on poverty are significantly different. By sharing ideas between students of both countries, participants succeeded to broaden their perspectives and found clues for resolving such problems. UK-Japan Student Conference gained independence from Bizjapan in FY2017 but cooperation between the two parties is still ongoing.

Y7 Summit Japan 2016

Activity Year: 2016

Vision

Based on the philosophy of "Reflecting youth's opinions in a changing world", people under 30 years old suggested alternative policies to G7 leaders prior to Ise-Shima G7 summit.

Meets Art Project

Activity Year: 2017

Vision

Under the idea that "bright students should learn contemporary art", this project offers students an opportunity to create artworks by themselves. Through this experience, students will be able to view "Contemporary Art" as one of the tools in their arsenal to use for communicating their thoughts.

What we did

Many assume that “Contemporary Art” is difficult to understand without really trying to understand it. To let students view “Contemporary Art” as one of their communication tools, we hosted art workshops to intelligent students. The workshops consist of 4 parts; “Introduction”, “Appreciation” ,” Production” and “Explanation”. Through those workshops, students learned that they can become Contemporary Artists themselves and experienced the joy and bitterness of expressing themselves through art.

Zero-ichi joshi project 2017

Activity Year: 2017

Vision

Helping young Japanese women to be able to make proactive and subjective selections of their life and career in any circumstances and making great success in their future.

What we did

About 15 Bizjapan members joined the Zeroichi program as staff members from July to Semptember, 2017. This program was organized by Global Shapers Yokohama Hub to which the founder of Bizjapan currently belongs. This program was tailored to 20 female college students selected from all over Japan. In the first half of this program, participants were given the opportunity to clarify their future visions through mentoring sessions, panel discussions with entrepreneurs, groupworks,etc. Next, they experienced the everyday life of social entrepreneurs who will be their role model. Finally, at the end of the program, they experienced “Zeroichi”, which means to start their very own projects from scratch.
